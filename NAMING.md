## Living / Organic

The core metaphor: memory as organism, not storage.

### Biological

- **Mycelium** — underground fungal network that connects, shares nutrients, self-heals
- **Rhizome** — root system that spreads, connects, has no center (Deleuze)
- **Pollen** — carrier of information, cross-fertilizes
- **Spore** — dormant knowledge that activates when needed
- **Symbiont** — organism that lives in mutual benefit
- **Biome** — living ecosystem
- **Hive** — collective intelligence, many agents, one memory
- **Colony** — self-organizing, emergent

### Growth / Flow

- **Pollinate** — cross-fertilize knowledge
- **Hydra** — regenerates, grows back stronger
- **Bloom** — knowledge flowering
- **Sap** — lifeblood flowing through the graph
- **Verdant** — green, alive, growing
- **Thrive** — living and growing

### Self-Maintaining

- **Prune** — cuts dead branches
- **Compost** — old knowledge feeds new
- **Cultivar** — cultivated variety
- **Tend** — active care

## From Wittgenstein

> *"The world is the totality of facts, not of things."*
> — Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus, 1.1

### Tractatus Concepts

| German | English | Concept |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Tatsache | Fact | Atomic truth |
| Sachverhalt | State of affairs | Atomic fact, smallest unit |
| Bild | Picture | Proposition as model of reality |
| Logischer Raum | Logical space | All possible states |
| Welt | World | Totality of facts |

### Names from Tractatus

- **Tatsache** — "fact" in German. Pronounced taht-zah-khuh.
- **Sachverhalt** — atomic fact. The irreducible unit. Too long.
- **Totality** — "the totality of facts"
- **Bild** — picture/model. Short, punchy.
- **Logischer** — logical. Logisch.

### Derived

- **Factotum** — "do everything" (Latin), but sounds like "facts + totum"
- **Faktum** — German spelling of fact
- **Tatsa** — shortened Tatsache
- **Sachver** — shortened Sachverhalt
